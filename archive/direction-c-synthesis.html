Direction C: Synthesis

The hybrid. Direction C takes the warmth of Direction A and the technical precision of Direction B, then adds a twist: an unexpected accent color that creates visual tension against the warm neutral base. The result is something that feels personal and distinctive without being quirky.

  • Warm-contemporary neutrals — similar warmth to Direction A but slightly more neutral (hue ~88, lower chroma) for a modern, less nostalgic feel
  • Deep teal accent (active) — the teal sits opposite warm tones on the color wheel, creating sophisticated tension. Two alternates shown: electric blue and muted crimson
  • Confident typography — Inter at weight 800 for headings creates bold presence; the dramatic contrast between heavy headings and calm body text IS the brand
  • Maximum whitespace — the most spacious of the three directions, giving every element room to breathe

Theming

Light and Dark

Toggle the theme using the button in the top-right corner. Dark mode uses warm dark backgrounds (hue ~90, chroma ~0.012) so the deep teal accent pops without a cool or clinical feel. Text stays warm off-white, borders remain subtle and warm.
